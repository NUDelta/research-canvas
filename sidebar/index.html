Design Arguments
  • Definition

    Design arguments are reasons for why a design may work. They provide a framework for analyzing, selecting, and evaluating ideas.

  • Format

    If you want to

    • design intervention X
    • for the purpose/function Y
    • in context Z,

    then you are best advised to

    • give that intervention the characteristics A, B, and C, and to
    • do that via affordances and procedures K, L, and M,
    • because of arguments P, Q, and R.
  • Component Definition

  • Example

    If you want to create an express train that connect the Pink and Green lines for the purpose of saving time riding the “L” for people transferring between the pink and green line in the context of Chicago public transportation , then you are best advised to move the train 5mph faster and have no transfer , and to do that via a new train with upgraded motor and having the train run between the pink and green lines because of a faster train will cover more distance in less time and no transfer means there is no transfer wait time for users.
